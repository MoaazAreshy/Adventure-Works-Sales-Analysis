# Adventure Works Sales Analysis

A **data analytics project built entirely using Microsoft Excel** to analyze sales performance, product profitability, and customer behavior using the Adventure Works dataset.
The project includes **data cleaning, exploratory data analysis (EDA), SQL-style analysis, data modeling using Power Pivot, and interactive dashboards for business insights.**

🔗 Project Repository
[https://github.com/MoaazAreshy/Adventure-Works-Sales-Analysis](https://github.com/MoaazAreshy/Adventure-Works-Sales-Analysis)

---

# Overview

This project analyzes the **Adventure Works sales dataset** to understand business performance across time, products, customers, and regions.

The analysis focuses on answering key questions such as:

* How sales and profit change over time
* Which products generate the most profit
* Customer contribution to overall revenue
* Profit distribution across regions
* Customer demographic influence on sales

All analysis and visualization were completed **using Excel only**, with **Power Pivot used for data modeling and calculations**.

# Dashboards

The project includes **two main dashboards**:

## 1. Time Analysis Dashboard

This dashboard focuses on **sales performance over time**.

Main insights include:

* Total revenue, profit, and transactions
* Monthly profit trends
* Profit contribution by weekdays
* Quarterly profit distribution
* Performance comparison across years
* Country-level filtering

The dashboard allows filtering by:

* Year
* Month
* Country

---

## 2. Detail Analysis Dashboard

This dashboard focuses on **products and customer insights**.

Key visualizations include:

* **Top 5 most profitable products**
* **Profit contribution of top customers**
* **Profit by product color**
* **Customer age group contribution**
* **Profit distribution by gender**
* **Regional profit contribution**
* **Product pricing categories**

Additional KPIs include:

* Total available products
* Sold vs unsold products
* Total customers
* Average customer age

---

# Results & Insights

Key findings from the analysis:

* A small group of products generates a significant share of total profit.
* Certain countries contribute the majority of revenue.
* Older customer segments contribute a large portion of profit.
* Profit varies significantly across months and quarters.
* Weekdays generate more profit compared to weekends.
